2121 Design Sight_

21_21 Design Sight is a design museum in Tokyo, Japan, known for exhibitions that explore the intersection of design, art, and everyday life. Its curatorial approach challenges conventional perspectives and highlights design’s influence on culture and society.

For this project, we developed cohesive branding materials for two simultaneous exhibitions—Another Kind of Art in the Main Gallery and Futures In-Sight in the Side Gallery. Each exhibition required a distinct visual identity while remaining unified under the museum’s overall branding, resulting in a system that balances contrast, clarity, and cohesion.

Outcome_

Another Kind of Art_

Another Kind of Art centers on the Japanese Mingei movement, which celebrates the beauty of handcrafted everyday objects made by anonymous artisans. The visual system uses a red and blue eclipse on a white background to convey cultural meaning and simplicity. Blue references the museum's logo, reinforcing brand continuity, while red connects to the Japanese national flag. The white background reflects the understated nature of Mingei, and slanted black typography introduces movement and a contemporary contrast, bridging tradition with modern design.

Futures In-Sight_

Futures In-Sight explores diverse perspectives on the future and how technology, global events, and human imagination shape what lies ahead. The visuals feature a dark background with white text to create a bold, futuristic atmosphere. The word "FUTURES" is emphasized through scale and a subtle blue outline, producing a shifting 3D effect that appears to expand or recede depending on perspective, reflecting the uncertainty and multiplicity of future visions.

Reflection_

This project highlighted the importance of designing a flexible visual system that supports contrasting narratives while maintaining cohesion. Balancing symbolic meaning, typographic hierarchy, and visual consistency strengthened the ability to translate conceptual themes into clear, intentional design outcomes.
