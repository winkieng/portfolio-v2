Holland Festival_

This project involved creating a promotional poster and supporting visuals for the 2023 Holland Festival. The design needed to incorporate principles inspired by Dan Friedman's Radical Modernism and Ellen Lupton's approach to typographic structure.

The goal was to merge expressive experimentation with clear visual communication, resulting in a poster system that felt energetic, cultural, and contemporary.

Outcome_

final_01 ×

The design approach involves inverting background colors to create an intriguing transparent effect. It incorporates a bird pattern with symbolic meanings such as freedom and liberation, as well as creativity and inspiration from various cultures and diverse music performances. The text varies in scale and boldness, with the word "festival" using transparency overlay and "holland" in a solid color to make it stand out.

Reflection_

This project highlighted the importance of translating design principles into practical visual decisions. Applying Dan Friedman's experimental approach pushed the use of colour inversion, scale play, and layered transparency, while Ellen Lupton's emphasis on clarity and typographic structure ensured the poster remained readable and organized.

Balancing these two directions required thoughtful refinement, especially in creating harmony between bold expression and functional hierarchy. The process strengthened the ability to analyze design philosophies and integrate them into a cohesive system, resulting in a poster that feels both energetic and intentional.
