Langley Farm Market_

Langley Farm Market is a long-standing local grocery store whose visual identity had remained unchanged for more than 20 years.

As the customer base expanded beyond its original 40+ demographic, the client commissioned a full brand refresh to modernize the store's appearance and better appeal to a younger generation, while still preserving the iconic deep red that long-time shoppers recognized and trusted.

The rebrand involved redesigning the logo, updating the colour and typography system, refreshing social media templates, improving in-store digital displays, and creating cohesive print materials. The new system introduced a more contemporary and approachable look while maintaining clarity, consistency, and recognizability across all touchpoints.

explanation ×

The original logo was familiar but hard to read. The digital version showed "LFM" with the smaller "Langley Farm Market" and a tagline, while the in-store version only had "LFM," making the full name unclear. A modern logo with clearer hierarchy was needed.

Customer's Goal_

1. Modernize the 20-year-old brand while preserving its recognizable deep-red heritage.

2. Refresh the visual identity to appeal to a younger demographic while remaining familiar to existing 40+ customers.

3. Improve readability and visual clarity across logo, typography, and colour usage.

4. Strengthen brand consistency and engagement across print materials, in-store displays, and social media.

Design Challenge_

• Updating a well-known local identity without disconnecting long-time customers who were accustomed to the original look.

• Addressing legibility issues, particularly the small "Langley Farm Market" text and the tagline, which were difficult to read in the original logo.

• Modernizing the visual language to feel more youthful and contemporary while maintaining instant recognizability.

• Ensuring the refreshed logo performs effectively on large signage, outdoor displays, and digital formats.

Rebranding_

Logo Redesigned_

Client's Requirement

To modernize the brand while still feeling familiar to customers, the client requested:

• A dominant red colour (with flexibility to explore new hues)

• Retaining the basket concept

• A more youthful yet trustworthy aesthetic

logo_exploration ×

Final Logo

After multiple rounds, the design evolved toward a warmer, more elegant direction:

• A serif font for Langley Farm introduces character and heritage

• A clean sans-serif for Market adds readability and balance

• The basket icon was softened with gentle curves for a more approachable feel

• Stroke widths and spacing were carefully adjusted for visibility on large exterior signage

COLOUR_

Because the client wanted to keep red as the dominant colour while exploring new red tones, the updated palette maintains heritage but adds flexibility:

• Reds: A range from deep heritage red to modern accents

• Beige: Adds warmth and a nostalgic, market-like character

• Dark Grey: Provides strong contrast for pricing, text, and digital clarity

Outcome_

The refreshed brand identity strengthened Langley Farm Market's presence across digital, print, and in-store touchpoints. The updated logo and structured design system improved clarity, consistency, and overall brand recognition.

Revised social media templates and content contributed to 4,000+ new followers (as of Dec 2024), supporting stronger online engagement. In-store signage, monitor screens, and promotional materials also became more cohesive and readable for customers of all ages.

The rebrand successfully balanced modernization with heritage—introducing a contemporary look without losing the familiarity valued by long-time shoppers.

Reflection_

Refreshing a 20-year-old brand required balancing modern appeal with long-standing customer familiarity. Keeping the signature red preserved the store's heritage, while updated typography, an expanded palette, and simplified visuals helped move the identity toward a more contemporary tone.

A key challenge was maintaining warmth and recognizability for long-time shoppers. Minimal icons, sketch-style graphics, and a cleaner serif direction allowed the brand to feel both familiar and refreshed.

The project strengthened the ability to update legacy brands thoughtfully—creating design systems that respect existing audiences while engaging younger generations across digital, print, and in-store environments.
